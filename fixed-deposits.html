The distinction we put first

A bank FD and an NBFC FD are not the same product.

They share the name, the mechanics and the tax treatment. They do not share the safety net.

DICGC-insured

Scheduled commercial bank FD

Issued by scheduled commercial banks, private banks and small finance banks. Deposits are insured by the Deposit Insurance and Credit Guarantee Corporation (DICGC) up to ₹5 lakh per depositor per bank — principal and accrued interest combined.

Typical 15-24m rate
7.1% – 7.6%
Insurance cover
Yes — ₹5L / depositor / bank
Regulator
RBI under BR Act 1949
Best for
Emergency fund, 6-12m float, capital preservation
NOT insured

NBFC public deposit

Issued by RBI-registered NBFCs holding a Certificate of Registration to accept public deposits. Higher coupon than bank FDs, but the deposit is not covered by DICGC or any other deposit-insurance scheme. Investor carries full credit risk of the NBFC.

Typical 15-36m rate
7.85% – 9.00%
Insurance cover
No
Regulator
RBI under RBI Act 1934, Chapter III-B
Best for
Yield uplift after bank FD exhausts, short tenor, top-rated NBFCs only
Mandatory RBI disclosure for NBFC public deposits. Per RBI notification, advertisements for NBFC deposits must carry this statement: "The deposits solicited are not insured and the repayment of deposits is not guaranteed by either the Central Government or the Reserve Bank of India." AnvithBizCap only distributes NBFC deposits with minimum investment-grade rating (AA or higher) and a verified Certificate of Registration.

How we think about FDs

Four moments when an FD is genuinely the right tool.

Fixed deposits are often chosen by default. We think they're optimal only in a narrow set of contexts — and wrong for wealth creation. Here are the four moments we actively recommend them.

01 · Liquidity layer

The ₹5 L emergency fund

Six months of household expenses. Always in bank FDs, split across two banks to stay under the ₹5 L DICGC cap per bank. Short tenure (6-12 months) with sweep-in facility for instant access.

02 · Rate-lock moment

Peak of the rate cycle

When the RBI repo rate has peaked and the yield curve begins flattening, 3–5 year FDs lock in rates that subsequently decline. Historically, an 18-month window around the peak is the best FD entry.

03 · Retirement income

The monthly-payout ladder

Retirees valuing predictability and capital preservation. A 5-rung FD ladder with monthly-payout mode smooths cash flow; the senior-citizen uplift typically adds 25–50 bps over the posted rate.

04 · Known liability

Goal with a fixed date

University fees in 18 months. A house down-payment in 2 years. When the horizon is short and the amount is critical, an FD removes market-timing risk entirely — at the cost of real-return drag.

The uncomfortable arithmetic. A 7.5% FD taxed at the 30% slab yields 5.25% post-tax. CPI inflation in the 4–6% range means your real return is 0–1% — effectively treading water. FDs are a tool for capital preservation, not wealth creation. We'd rather tell you that upfront than have you figure it out after 10 years.
Tax treatment

Interest is taxed — at your slab.

Unlike equity mutual funds, there is no concessional rate for FD interest. This is the main reason HNIs diversify away from FDs into debt funds (earlier) and corporate bonds.

How taxed
Interest is added to your total income and taxed at your applicable slab.
TDS trigger (bank)
Interest > ₹40,000 per year (₹50,000 for senior citizens) — 10% TDS deducted by the bank. Form 15G / 15H available for no-tax individuals.
TDS trigger (NBFC)
Interest > ₹5,000 per year — 10% TDS deducted by the NBFC.
Reporting
Interest is pre-filled in your AIS. We send a consolidated year-end certificate (aggregated across all issuers distributed through us).
Premature withdrawal
Typically 0.5–1.0% penalty on the applicable rate for the run tenure. Some NBFC products have a hard lock (12 months for NBFC public deposits per RBI Master Directions).
Section 80C
Only 5-year tax-saver bank FDs qualify — ₹1.5 L aggregate deduction with 80C bucket.
Common questions

Before you click book.

How do I know an NBFC is safe enough to deposit with?
Four screens: (1) Verify the NBFC holds an RBI Certificate of Registration to accept public deposits — we confirm this before adding any issuer to our shelf; (2) Check the public credit rating from CRISIL / ICRA / CARE — we distribute only AA or better; (3) Read the last two quarterly results for NPA ratio, capital adequacy and asset quality; (4) Check the NBFC's own deposit ceiling — they cannot hold deposits beyond 1.5x their net-owned funds. We do this screening for every issuer on our shelf, refreshed quarterly.
Should I split ₹10 L across two banks to stay under DICGC?
If preserving every rupee of principal is paramount — yes. DICGC insurance is per depositor per bank, up to ₹5 L (principal + interest). Splitting ₹10 L into two banks gives you effective insurance on the full ₹10 L. For most investors with a diversified portfolio, this is overkill on a large, well-rated private bank; but for the household emergency fund specifically, we routinely advise the split.
Cumulative vs. monthly payout — which is better?
Cumulative compounds quarterly and pays out principal + full accumulated interest at maturity — higher effective yield, but no cash flow in between. Monthly payout gives you a cheque every month but compounds less (simple interest on the principal). For retirees with income need, monthly is correct. For anyone reinvesting the interest anyway, cumulative is mathematically superior.
What happens if I need the money before maturity?
Most bank FDs allow premature withdrawal with a penalty of 0.5–1.0% on the rate applicable for the actual run tenure. For NBFC public deposits, RBI Master Directions impose a hard minimum-lock of 3 months; withdrawal between 3–6 months earns no interest; between 6–12 months earns 2% below the FD rate. Read the specific issuer's premature-withdrawal schedule before booking — we display it on the shortlist card.
Can a company or HUF invest in these FDs?
Yes. Company FDs (NBFC public deposits) and corporate bank FDs are standard treasury instruments. For entity onboarding we need the entity PAN, incorporation certificate, last two years of audited financials, board resolution authorizing the investment and signatories, and KYC of authorized signatories. Our Corporate Treasury desk handles the full entity workflow.
