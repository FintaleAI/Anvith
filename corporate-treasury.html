The treasury framework

Four buckets. One policy.

We don't start with products. We start with the cash-flow calendar. The question is what portion of float is genuinely required within 7 days, 30 days, 6 months, or 2 years — and we allocate by tier.

Tier 1 · T+0

Operating float

Immediate payables, payroll, GST. Kept in current account + overnight / liquid MF sweeps. Yield: 6.4–6.8%.

Tier 2 · T+1–T+30

Contingency

Ultra-short-term debt funds, arbitrage funds, money-market funds. Exit in 1-7 days without load. Yield: 6.9–7.3%.

Tier 3 · 30-day–1-year

Surplus

Corporate bank FDs, rated NBFC FDs (short tenor), ultra-ST and short-term debt funds. Yield: 7.1–8.2%.

Tier 4 · 1–3 years

Strategic

Laddered corporate bonds (AAA), 2–3-year NBFC FDs. Yield: 7.8–8.9%. Locked duration; not touched for ops.

Entity KYC workflow

Documentation we'll need from you.

Under PMLA 2002 and the KRA / CKYC framework, corporate treasury onboarding requires a deeper documentation set than individual KYC. We handle the filing, you provide the source documents.

Identity

Entity proof

  • Certificate of Incorporation (for Pvt Ltd / LLP / Public Ltd)
  • Partnership deed (for partnership firms)
  • Trust deed (for trusts)
  • Entity PAN & GST registration
  • MOA & AOA (for companies)
  • Latest two years' audited financial statements
Authorization

Decision & signatory

  • Board resolution authorizing investment in mutual funds / bonds / FDs, specifying authorized signatories (template provided)
  • List of authorised signatories with specimen signatures
  • KYC of all authorized signatories (PAN + Aadhaar)
  • Cancelled corporate cheque or recent bank statement
Beneficial ownership

UBO declaration

  • List of Ultimate Beneficial Owners (10%+ ownership or control, per PMLA rules)
  • KYC of each UBO
  • FATCA / CRS declaration if entity has foreign UBOs or US connections
  • Shareholding pattern (for companies) / partnership structure
Operational

Settlement & reporting

  • Corporate Demat account (we open if needed, via partner DP)
  • Investment policy statement (we draft jointly)
  • Reporting preferences: single / multi-recipient, format, frequency
  • Nominee / successor-in-interest designations where applicable

Why a desk, not a software

Because a treasury decision is not a product decision.

Self-serve treasury apps exist. They're perfectly adequate for parking last month's GST in a liquid fund. What they cannot do: model your cash-flow seasonality, read your MSA terms, translate the CFO's loss-tolerance into an allocation policy, and sit in a board call when the auditor asks why you hold AA NBFC paper. Those require a desk with a human on it.

Policy before product

We co-draft an investment policy statement with the CFO and the board — what's permissible, what's out of bounds, what the approval workflow is. Every subsequent transaction is tested against that policy.

Calendar-aware

We model your advance-tax dates, salary cycles, vendor payment seasonality and dividend payout schedules. Allocation is never mapped to the calendar — not to a one-size-fits-all model.

Human escalation

Rate-movement above X bps, rating downgrade, or any issuer on a watchlist triggers a same-day RM call. You are never the last to know.
