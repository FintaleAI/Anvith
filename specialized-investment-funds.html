Specialized
Investment Funds.
The middle ground India was waiting for.

SEBI's Specialized Investment Fund framework — effective 1 April 2025 — establishes a new regulated asset class sitting between traditional mutual funds and Portfolio Management Services. For the first time, Indian affluent investors can access long-short equity, dynamic debt and hybrid derivative strategies at a ₹10 lakh entry threshold, with the liquidity and trustee oversight of a mutual fund but the strategic latitude of an AIF.

The minimum investment is cumulative across all SIF strategies managed by a single AMC, applied at the PAN level. It is not a per-scheme or per-transaction test. Our order-management logic enforces this gate before any subscription or redemption — a partial redemption that would drop the aggregate below ₹10 L is rejected at the intent stage.

Worked illustration

Priya subscribes ₹7 L to AMC-A's long-short equity SIF, then ₹4 L to AMC-A's dynamic debt SIF. Aggregate = ₹11 L ✓ compliant. Six months later she wishes to redeem ₹2 L. New aggregate = ₹9 L ✗ rejected — she must either redeem ≥ ₹10 L (full exit from one strategy) or keep aggregate above ₹10 L. Her second AMC-B holding is unaffected — the gate is per-AMC.

The structural gap

For years, Indian wealth had a missing middle.

Traditional mutual funds are constrained in what they can do. Portfolio Management Services start at ₹50 lakh. Alternative Investment Funds require ₹1 crore. The serious but not-ultra-wealthy Indian investor had no way to access the strategies that high-net-worth families had used globally for decades. SEBI's 2025 SIF framework fills exactly that gap.

Suitability

SIF is not automatically the right product — even for investors who qualify.

Eligibility (₹10 L per PAN per AMC) is a floor, not a signal. Before proceeding, we look for six confirmations in your risk profile — and we walk away from the engagement if the picture isn't clear.

01

Loss tolerance

Comfort with 10–25% drawdowns in a 3-year window, particularly from long-short strategies during equity rallies where the short book loses.

02

Horizon

Minimum 3-year commitment. Derivative strategies need time to realize their thesis; short-term liquidity access should be handled elsewhere.

03

Existing allocation

A core MF book of ₹30–50 L already in place. SIF is a satellite, not a foundation. We cap SIF exposure at 20–25% of investable net worth.

04

Tax capacity

SIFs are taxed at the underlying-asset rate — equity SIF gets equity-MF tax, debt SIF gets debt-MF tax. Short-position gains may be speculative. We model scenarios before recommending.

Frequently asked

Affluent desk FAQs.

How is a SIF different from a PMS?
Three material differences. First: threshold. PMS starts at ₹50 L; SIF at ₹10 L. Second: structure. PMS manages a separate bespoke portfolio in your own Demat; SIF pools investor capital in a unit-trust structure (you hold units, not securities). Third: liquidity. PMS typically has monthly redemption windows; SIF is daily or weekly. What they share is strategic latitude — long-short, derivatives, concentration. For most investors in the ₹10 L–₹50 L affluent band, SIF is the more liquid, more transparent, more regulated alternative.
Is SIF taxation the same as mutual funds?
Yes, with nuance. SIFs are regulated under the Mutual Fund Regulations (Chapter XVI-B), so the tax treatment follows the underlying-asset classification: equity SIF (65%+ equity) gets equity taxation (12.5% LTCG over ₹1.25 L), debt SIF gets slab-rate income, hybrid SIF follows its classification. Gains from derivative positions are typically treated as speculative or business income if they dominate, so we run a scenario with your tax adviser before subscription. Short-position realized losses can be set off against equity capital gains — a meaningful advantage.
What does the ₹10 L threshold actually cap — my investment, or something else?
It caps your minimum aggregate holding at a PAN level across all SIF strategies of the same AMC. You can subscribe ₹5 L to Strategy A and ₹5 L to Strategy B of AMC-X and you meet the floor. You cannot have ₹7 L only across AMC-X's SIF strategies. Our OMS validates this before every subscription and redemption; a redemption that would push aggregate below ₹10 L is rejected. The gate resets if the aggregate rises and falls within a given day due to NAV changes — it's only the user-initiated transactions that are validated.
Can I SIP into a SIF?
Yes, provided each SIP installment keeps the aggregate above the ₹10 L floor. In practice, most SIF subscriptions are lumpsum — but for investors adding to an existing holding that is already well above the threshold, SIP is permitted. Minimum SIP amounts vary by AMC, typically ₹1 L/month upwards.
Is AnvithBizCap certified to distribute SIFs?
Yes. Our designated key personnel have passed the NISM Series-XIII: Common Derivatives Certification Examination (the mandatory certification for SIF distribution). Our AMFI SIF registration was processed through CAMS and is currently active. Distribution eligibility is verifiable — we list the key-person NISM registration numbers on our compliance page.
How many SIFs should I own?
Usually 1 or 2, never more than 3. SIF strategies aim for uncorrelated return streams relative to the equity index, so a second SIF of a different archetype (e.g., long-short + market-neutral, or equity + credit-rates) can diversify. Beyond three, the strategies tend to neutralize each other and you end up with a high-cost index replica. We model correlations during construction.

Specialized Investment Funds (SIFs) are a regulated asset class introduced by SEBI through the February 27, 2025 circular, effective 1 April 2025. Minimum investment is ₹10,00,000 per PAN across all SIF strategies of a single AMC. SIFs may employ derivative strategies (including short-selling) up to 25% of net assets for non-hedging purposes, and may concentrate positions in ways that ordinary mutual funds cannot. They therefore carry higher risk than standard mutual funds — including variable liquidity risk, concentration risk, and potential amplified drawdowns in adverse market conditions.

Only distributors with NISM Series-XIII certification and active AMFI SIF registration may distribute SIFs. Performance of any SIF strategy depends on the asset manager's execution and market conditions; past performance of the asset manager or of affiliated mutual fund schemes is not an indicator of future SIF performance.
