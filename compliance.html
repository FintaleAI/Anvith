Section 02

AMFI identity & advertising disclosures

Per the AMFI Master Circular for Mutual Fund Distributors, AnvithBizCap displays the statutory tagline "AMFI-registered Mutual Fund Distributor" alongside our registered name, ARN Code and validity period in the footer of every page of this website, in every email signature, and in every printed marketing collateral. The tagline is rendered in a minimum font size equivalent to 12 points, as required by AMFI.

We also observe AMFI's nomenclature restrictions. We do not use titles such as "Independent Financial Adviser," "Investment Adviser," "Wealth Adviser," or any other term that implies independent advisory services, because those titles are restricted to entities holding a SEBI Investment Adviser registration. AnvithBizCap operates as a distributor and executor; where a client requires a flat-fee advisory relationship, we refer them to a SEBI RIA partner and disclose the referral economics (if any).

Under AMFI's code, distributors are encouraged to provide broad educational content and must refrain from making scheme-specific recommendations or performance predictions on public platforms. All content on our Reading Room and social channels is accordingly educational. Scheme-specific shortlists are shared only within a recorded suitability engagement, with the investor's written risk profile on record.

Section 04

Risk disclaimers by product

4.1 Mutual Funds

Mutual fund investments are subject to market risks, read all scheme-related documents carefully. The past performance of mutual fund schemes is not an indicator of future performance. Mutual funds, like any market-linked investment, can gain or lose value. Equity schemes carry higher volatility and can experience drawdowns of 30–50% during bear markets; debt schemes carry credit and interest-rate risks; liquid and overnight schemes can also see short-term NAV declines in stress conditions. Please read the Scheme Information Document (SID), Key Information Memorandum (KIM) and Statement of Additional Information (SAI) before investing.

4.2 Corporate Bonds

Corporate bonds carry four distinct risks: credit (issuer may default on coupon or principal), interest-rate (market-to-market decline when yields rise), liquidity (secondary market volumes can be thin), and reinvestment (coupons may be reinvested at lower rates if market yields fall). Ratings are opinions issued by credit rating agencies and can be downgraded mid-life. Bond distribution is routed through a SEBI-registered Online Bond Platform Provider (OBPP) partner; AnvithBizCap does not itself operate an OBPP. Investor funds settle directly through the exchange clearing corporation (ICCL / NSE Clearing) — never through AnvithBizCap's accounts.

4.3 Fixed Deposits

Bank fixed deposits are covered by the Deposit Insurance and Credit Guarantee Corporation (DICGC) up to ₹5 lakh per depositor per bank, including principal and accrued interest. NBFC public deposits are not insured. Per RBI mandate, the statutory warning for NBFC deposits is: "The deposits solicited are not insured and the repayment of deposits is not guaranteed by either the Central Government or the Reserve Bank of India." AnvithBizCap distributes only NBFC public deposits with minimum investment-grade rating (AA or higher) from NBFCs holding a valid RBI Certificate of Registration.

4.4 Sovereign Gold Bonds

Sovereign Gold Bonds are issued by the Reserve Bank of India on behalf of the Government of India. They are exposed to gold price risk — the value at redemption or on secondary-market sale depends on the prevailing gold price, which can decline. The capital-gains tax exemption at 8-year maturity applies only to the original subscriber who holds the bond to maturity, or who exits through the RBI's Year-5+ premature-redemption window. Secondary-market purchases on the exchange do not qualify for the exemption. The 2.5% coupon interest is taxable at the investor's slab rate.

4.5 Specialized Investment Funds

Specialized Investment Funds (SIFs) are a regulated asset class introduced by SEBI through the February 27, 2025 circular, effective 1 April 2025. The minimum investment is ₹10,00,000 per PAN across all SIF strategies of a single AMC. SIFs may employ derivative strategies (including short-selling) up to 25% of net assets for non-hedging purposes, and may concentrate positions in ways that ordinary mutual funds cannot. They therefore carry higher risk than standard mutual funds — including variable liquidity risk, concentration risk and potentially amplified drawdowns in adverse market conditions.

Only distributors holding a valid ARN/EUIN, with individual distributor personnel who have passed the NISM Series-XIII (Common Derivatives) Certification and who have obtained a separate SIF registration with AMFI, are permitted to distribute SIFs. AnvithBizCap's NISM Series-XIII certification is active for two key personnel, and our AMFI SIF registration has been processed via CAMS.

No product is risk-free. No projection is a guarantee. Every figure, return band, yield indication or maturity value shown anywhere on this website is illustrative — derived either from historical data, user-entered assumptions in calculators, or issuer-published rate cards. None of it constitutes an offer, recommendation, or guarantee. If you are uncertain, speak to your RM or consult an independent tax / legal / financial adviser.
Section 05

KYC, AML & PMLA record-keeping

Onboarding follows the SEBI / RBI unified KYC framework and is PMLA-compliant. Individual onboarding uses PAN-centric KYC with CKYCR reuse where available, Aadhaar-based e-KYC or Aadhaar Paperless Offline e-KYC (XML) via UIDAI, video-IPV, and bank-account verification via penny drop.

For entity onboarding (companies, LLPs, partnerships, trusts, Section-8) we capture Certificate of Incorporation / partnership deed / trust deed, entity PAN, board resolution or equivalent authorization, list of authorized signatories with their KYC, list of Ultimate Beneficial Owners (10%+ ownership or control per PMLA rules) with their KYC, and FATCA / CRS declarations where foreign connections exist.

Under the Prevention of Money Laundering Act 2002, we maintain transaction records for five years from the date of transaction, and identity / account-file / business-correspondence records for five years after the business relationship ends or the account is closed. The retention mirrors the RBI's KYC Master Directions obligation on regulated entities.

All onboarding documentation is electronically signed using a CCA-licensed eSign provider with full audit trail (Aadhaar-based OTP verification, hash-based document signing). Paper signatures are accepted only where the client specifically requests them.

Section 06

Privacy — Digital Personal Data Protection Act, 2023

AnvithBizCap processes personal data of identifiable natural persons, and has accordingly aligned its data-handling architecture to the Digital Personal Data Protection Act 2023 and the Digital Personal Data Protection Rules 2025 notified in November 2025.

6.1 Your rights

  • Access — you can request a summary of the personal data we hold on you
  • Correction — you can request correction or updating of inaccurate data
  • Erasure — you can request deletion, subject to statutory retention requirements under SEBI, RBI and PMLA
  • Withdrawal of consent — for processing activities that are purely consent-based (marketing, newsletter, cross-selling). Withdrawal does not apply to data required for statutory compliance (e.g., PAN and KYC records we are required to retain under PMLA)
  • Grievance — you can raise a privacy complaint with our Data Protection Officer; unresolved complaints can be escalated to the Data Protection Board of India

6.2 Our Unified Consent Management

Our consent architecture captures granular, step-wise consent across the user journey. At each point of data collection — onboarding, marketing opt-in, third-party data sharing, WhatsApp communication — we make the specific purpose explicit and capture an immutable timestamped consent log. The consent ledger can be exported and is presented as part of your annual KYC re-certification.

We clearly distinguish data processing that is consent-based from data processing that is statutorily required. For example: withdrawing consent for marketing communications is executed immediately and has no operational impact. Withdrawing consent for KYC-record retention would trigger the statutory-requirement path — we inform you that withdrawal will result in immediate suspension of transaction ability, because we are required by PMLA and SEBI to continue holding those records.

6.3 Breach notification

Under the DPDP Act, we are obligated to notify the Data Protection Board of India and affected Data Principals in the event of a personal data breach, regardless of whether the breach resulted in immediate harm. Our incident-response playbook triggers this notification within the timelines prescribed.

6.4 Data Protection Officer

Our Data Protection Officer is Raghav Nair, Head of Compliance. Contact: dpo@anvithbizcap.in. The DPO is also responsible for responding to SEBI / RBI / CERT-In data requests within their prescribed turnaround windows.

Section 07

Cybersecurity — SEBI CSCRF 2025 alignment

AnvithBizCap has architected its technology stack in alignment with the SEBI Cybersecurity and Cyber Resilience Framework 2025, which moved Indian SEBI-regulated entities from perimeter-defence to a Zero-Trust posture.

7.1 Identity & access

Role-Based Access Control across client portal, API gateway and administrative back-end. Multi-Factor Authentication is mandatory for every staff and client session. Administrative rights on employee endpoints are blocked by default, granted only through a Privileged Access Management tool for strictly need-to-know durations. User access reviews are conducted automatically every 90 days; dormant accounts are revoked immediately.

7.2 Cryptography & storage

All Personally Identifiable Information (PII), KYC documents and historical transaction logs are encrypted at rest using AES-256 and in transit using TLS 1.2 or higher. Session tokens are issued per-request and never stored in HTML hidden fields, browser cookies or local storage. Keys are managed via a cloud KMS (AWS KMS) with rotation policies enforced.

7.3 Security operations

A 24×7 Security Operations Centre is deployed via a specialist managed service provider, with continuous threat monitoring, anomaly detection and automated incident response. CERT-In cyber incident reporting obligations (6-hour reporting window) are integrated into the incident runbook. ICT logs are retained in India for 180 days in line with CERT-In directions.

7.4 Data classification & regulatory availability

Data stores are tagged Public / Internal / Confidential / Restricted. Immutable backups are retained such that IT and cybersecurity data can be retrieved and made available to SEBI, CERT-In or other authorized government agencies within 48 hours of a formal request.

7.5 Vendor & third-party risk

Third-party SaaS providers and cloud hosts are subject to annual vendor security assessments. All SLAs with material vendors include CSCRF-aligned security clauses and require independent verification of ISO 27001 or equivalent compliance.

7.6 Testing & audit

An annual Vulnerability Assessment and Penetration Test (VAPT) is conducted by an independent CERT-In empanelled firm. A summary of findings (excluding sensitive technical detail) is shared with clients as part of the yearly compliance update.

Section 09

Grievance redressal & escalation

Our grievance redressal framework is published prominently, because it is one of the most important elements of investor protection. We operate a four-step escalation path.

  1. Your relationship manager. Most operational matters are resolved the same day. Your RM's direct line and email are in the onboarding pack.
  2. Compliance & Grievance Officer. Raghav Nair, Head of Compliance. Email: grievance@anvithbizcap.in. Formal grievances are acknowledged within 2 business days and resolved within 30 days.
  3. SEBI SCORES. If the grievance is not resolved within 30 days or the resolution is unsatisfactory, escalate to SEBI's SCORES portal: scores.sebi.gov.in.
  4. Online Dispute Resolution (ODR). SEBI's ODR framework for market-participant disputes: smartodr.in.

We maintain an immutable grievance log with timestamps for every complaint, communication, and resolution. A half-yearly summary of grievance statistics (total received, resolved within SLA, escalated) is published on this page at the end of each half-year and is also shared with SEBI in our periodic returns.

Section 10

SIF-specific compliance

Specialized Investment Fund distribution involves heightened regulatory requirements compared to standard mutual funds. We enforce the following as part of every SIF engagement.

  • Distributor qualification. Only NISM Series-XIII certified personnel distribute SIFs. Currently certified: Anvith Krishnan and Kavya Sastry.
  • Standalone risk profiling. A SEBI-required SIF-specific risk profile is captured before any SIF product is discussed, separate from the general MF risk profile.
  • Threshold enforcement. The ₹10,00,000 per-PAN-per-AMC minimum is validated pre-trade at our OMS level. Redemptions that would drop the aggregate below the floor are rejected with an explanatory note.
  • Bi-monthly disclosures. Portfolio and derivative-exposure disclosures published by the AMC by the 10th of the following month (for periods ending May, Jul, Sep, Nov, Jan, Mar) are surfaced on your logged-in dashboard.
  • Written strategy memo. A 2–3 page memo mapping eligible SIF strategies to the client's mandate is produced before any subscription, with scenario analysis and expense breakdown.
  • No mass marketing. SIFs are not promoted on social media or public webinars. Strategy-specific content is shared only under a suitability framework.
