How bond distribution is regulated — and why we partner, rather than operate an OBPP ourselves. SEBI's 2022 Online Bond Platform Providers framework requires any digital platform facilitating sale of listed bonds to be registered as an OBPP under a recognized stock exchange's debt segment. AnvithBizCap does not operate an independent OBPP; we distribute bonds through a SEBI-registered OBPP partner (details shared on onboarding). Investor funds settle directly through the exchange clearing corporation — never through our accounts — which eliminates counterparty risk. SEBI has publicly cautioned investors against unregistered bond platforms; our partnership is disclosed in your transaction confirmation.

Bond mechanics

Five numbers, before you buy.

Every bond on our shelf comes with a standardised fact card. We refuse to distribute any instrument where these five numbers can't be verified against the issue memorandum.

01

Yield to Maturity

The true return if held to maturity, accounting for current price, coupon and time. The coupon rate is not the yield — a 9% coupon bought at a premium may yield only 7%.

02

Credit rating

CRISIL, ICRA, CARE or India Ratings. We display the rating rationale and any recent watchlist changes. Ratings can be downgraded mid-life — we alert you within 24 hours.

03

Seniority

Secured vs. unsecured vs. subordinated. In bankruptcy, secured bonds are paid first from specified assets. Subordinated (AT1, Tier-2) bonds can be written down.

04

Call & put options

Some bonds can be called by the issuer (you lose the income stream) or put by the holder (you can exit early). We show the full call/put schedule with trigger dates.

05

Listing & liquidity

Listed on NSE/BSE debt segment. We indicate the last-30-day traded volume — a bond with near-zero secondary volume may be difficult to exit at a fair price.

Strategy · Laddering

A rolling income machine, not a one-time bet.

Rather than locking a single bond at today's rate, we construct laddered portfolios — bonds with staggered maturities — so that a portion matures every year and can be redeployed at the prevailing rate. This dampens interest-rate risk and creates a predictable annual cash flow.

Why ladder?

You can't time rates. Don't try.

If you lock a 10-year bond at today's 7.6% and rates rise to 9%, you are stuck below the market. If rates fall to 6%, you celebrated too early — but the issuer may call the bond. A 5-rung ladder always has a fresh deployment next year.

  • · Income smoothing across the rate cycle
  • · Automatic reinvestment discipline
  • · Partial liquidity — one rung redeems each year
  • · Lower duration risk than a bullet portfolio
The honest risk conversation

Bonds are not risk-free. Here's what can go wrong.

Corporate bonds carry four distinct risks — each separately priced by the market. We prefer to spell them out rather than have the call-centre explain a default three years in.

Credit risk

The issuer may default on coupon or principal. AAA-rated Indian corporates have historically defaulted < 0.05% over 5-year windows, but DHFL (2019) and IL&FS (2018) are live reminders that ratings are opinions, not guarantees. We monitor watchlists weekly.

Interest-rate risk

When market yields rise, the mark-to-market price of your existing bond falls. If you hold to maturity you still receive your YTM; but if you need to sell mid-life, you may realize a capital loss. Longer-tenor bonds carry more duration risk.

Liquidity risk

Secondary-market volumes in Indian corporate bonds are thin. You may not find a buyer at theoretical fair value on the day you need to exit. We prefer bonds with traded volume in the last 30 days, and we size your position accordingly.

Reinvestment risk

If rates fall during the life of the bond, the coupons you receive must be reinvested at lower rates — reducing your actual compounded return vs. the quoted YTM. This is the flip side of interest-rate risk, and it's what laddering partly addresses.

Questions we hear

Common questions.

Most bond-related anxieties, in five concise answers.

Do I need a Demat account to buy bonds?
Yes. Listed corporate bonds settle in dematerialized form — they cannot be held in physical certificates. If you don't already have a Demat account with NSDL or CDSL, we open one through a partner DP during onboarding. The process is fully paperless and takes 24–48 hours.
Is a tax-free PSU bond actually tax-free?
Yes — the coupon interest from tax-free PSU bonds (NHAI, REC, PFC, IRFC tax-free issues) is fully exempt under Section 10(15)(iv)(h) of the Income Tax Act. However, any capital gain if you sell the bond on the exchange before maturity is taxable as LTCG (12.5% after 12 months) or STCG (at slab). Tax-free bonds issued in 2012–16 at 6–8% tax-free coupons are especially valuable for investors in the 30% slab, where the taxable-equivalent yield is 8.6–11.4%.
What happens if the NBFC I invested in defaults?
A secured bondholder's claim is senior to equity and subordinated debt. You join a resolution process — either IBC-driven bankruptcy, or a scheme of arrangement — and typically receive recovery over 2–5 years at some fraction of the principal. Historical recovery rates on Indian corporate bond defaults are 40–70% for senior secured, 10–30% for subordinated. This is why we cap any single AA/AA+ issuer at 10% of the portfolio and avoid high-yield paper entirely for income-dependent clients.
Can I exit before maturity if I need the money?
Listed bonds can be sold on the NSE or BSE debt segment through your OBPP order window. Whether you get a fair price depends on the bond's liquidity. For thinly-traded issues, the bid-ask spread can be 1–3% — meaning you may give up a month's coupon to exit early. We prefer to build ladders precisely to avoid this scenario; at least one rung matures each year without any market exit.
How is bond income taxed?
Coupon interest is taxable at your income-tax slab (except the specific tax-free PSU bonds above). Capital gains on sale before maturity: STCG at slab if held < 12 months, LTCG at 12.5% if held ≥ 12 months (post July-2024 amendment). TDS applies above ₹5,000 annual interest per issuer. We send a year-end interest and capital-gains summary formatted for direct ITR import.
