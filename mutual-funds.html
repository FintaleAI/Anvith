Selection methodology

Seven screens before a scheme ever reaches your shortlist.

We don't rank by 1-year returns — the single most common error retail investors make. Our internal model pre-filters the eligible universe by seven durability signals, weights them, and re-runs monthly.

Screen 01

Vintage & continuity

Minimum 5-year track record through at least one drawdown cycle. AMC stability and fund-manager tenure.

Screen 02

Rolling returns

We look at 3-, 5- and 7-year rolling periods — not point-in-time. Consistency beats one-shot outperformance.

Screen 03

Risk-adjusted

Sortino, capture ratios, maximum drawdown. A 14% return with 40% drawdown fails the screen.

Screen 04

Expense discipline

TER relative to category median. We won't distribute schemes whose expense ratio has drifted upward without cause.

Screen 05

Portfolio hygiene

Concentration, churn, category drift. An equity fund with 30% cash isn't an equity fund.

Screen 06

Liquidity resilience

Scheme AUM, redemption experience during stress events (Mar-2020, Oct-2018 IL&FS), and credit quality in debt.

Screen 07

Regulatory standing

AMC compliance history, SEBI observations, and any material changes in investment mandate or trustee structure.

Output

Shortlist, not recommendation

A ranked universe of 30–40 schemes. Your final selection is made in conversation, with suitability captured in writing.

Common questions

Before the call.

The five questions every first-time mutual fund investor asks us, answered in the same tone we'd use on the phone.

Why do you distribute regular plans and not direct plans?
Regular plans allow us to sustain a fully-staffed servicing desk — compliance, onboarding, reporting, grievance — at zero direct cost to the investor. Over a 15-year SIP, the TER differential is real and visible; whether the judgment, rebalancing and service we provide offsets it is a question every investor should answer for themselves. For investors who prefer direct-plan economics with transparent flat-fee advisory, we refer to a SEBI RIA partner.
How does the money move — do you hold any of it?
No. All subscription amounts route from your bank account directly to the AMC's scheme collection account through BSE StAR MF's clearing system or the RTA platform. Redemption proceeds credit directly to your registered bank account. AnvithBizCap never operates a pooled investor account and never collects cheques or transfers in our name.
What KYC do I need to begin?
PAN, Aadhaar (for e-KYC), a cancelled cheque or recent bank statement, and a live selfie for video-IPV. If you are already KYC-registered with a KRA (CAMS, CVL, NDML, Karvy, NSE), we can pull your status via the CKYC registry and you typically begin investing the same day. NRIs require additional documentation — passport, visa, overseas address proof, and FATCA declaration.
Can I move an existing portfolio over to AnvithBizCap?
Yes. You change the ARN of record through a simple Mutual Fund Change of Distributor (MFCOD) form, which we provide. No redemption, no tax event, no exit load — your investment continues; only the servicing distributor changes. We recommend doing this only after a portfolio review, because in some cases the existing allocation should be restructured first.
What happens if I want to stop a SIP or exit a fund?
SIP cancellations are processed online in one click through our portal; a mandate stop reaches the AMC within one clearing cycle. Redemptions settle T+1 for equity schemes, T+1 for most debt schemes, same-day for liquid. Any applicable exit load (usually 1% if held under 12 months) is disclosed before confirmation. We'll also flag any tax implication before you click redeem.
Do you give scheme-specific recommendations on this website?
No — and we won't. AMFI's distributor code prohibits scheme-specific recommendations on public channels, because a recommendation without a suitability assessment is a regulatory violation. Our public content is restricted to education. Scheme-level shortlists are shared only in a private, KYC-onboarded session with your written risk profile on record.
