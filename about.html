The origin

Three questions the founders couldn't answer, even after a decade in the industry.

Anvith and Kavya spent their early careers on the institutional side of Indian wealth — one as a fixed-income trader at a private bank, the other as a fund selection analyst at a large RIA. The questions they couldn't answer in 2018 weren't technical. They were structural.

Why did the family-friend distributor push the same three schemes to every client, regardless of goal? Why was the commission structure the single least-discussed element of any product conversation? Why did a ₹70-lakh portfolio still have the same generic "3-fund" construction as a ₹5-lakh starter?

Distribution in India had confused "scale" with "sameness." We wanted to build the opposite: a small book, carefully constructed files, and the patience to sit out a product cycle.

They incorporated AnvithBizCap in August 2019 with an ARN, a two-person desk, and a single rule: no prospect becomes a client without a written brief on record. That rule is still in force in 2026, when the book has grown to ₹612 crore. The brief has grown too — it now covers tax residency, household UBO, succession intent, and multi-account consolidation — but it remains the first deliverable of every engagement.

Operating principles

Six rules the partners will not negotiate on.

Some of these cost us revenue. All of them make the firm sleep at night. We share them upfront because they often determine, more than any product feature, whether we are the right fit for your household.

01

We never collect client funds

All subscriptions route through regulated rails — BSE StAR MF, exchange clearing corporations, AMC escrow, issuer-bank accounts. We do not operate any pooled investor account, and you will never be asked to transfer money to an AnvithBizCap name.

02

Scheme recommendations are private

Per AMFI's distributor code, we make no scheme-specific recommendations on public pages, social media or open-audience webinars. Shortlists are shared only within a recorded suitability engagement.

03

Commissions are disclosed, in full

Our complete commission schedule — every AMC, every category, every trail band — is published on our compliance page and included in the onboarding pack. Volume bonuses, if ever accepted, would be disclosed individually.

04

One RM per household

No call tree, no relationship-roulette. The person who took your intake call still handles your file five years later. If the RM leaves the firm, we personally introduce your replacement before any transition.

05

Walking away is on the menu

If the conversation reveals that you need a fee-only SEBI RIA, or a portfolio size that warrants a PMS, we refer you out. We would rather lose the mandate than take one that doesn't fit.

06

We write it down

Every material decision — suitability conclusion, product shortlist, tax position, rebalance rationale — is captured in an email you can search five years from now. The audit trail is also what we'd want, if we were the client.
